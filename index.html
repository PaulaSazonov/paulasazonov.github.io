My story

I have a Master's degree in English Philology from Helsinki University, but found myself wanting to change lanes into a career in IT. I'm an ambitious and hardworking spirit who's never afraid of a little challenge, so I applied in an intensive learning program in programming. After multiple years of academia, I found the idea of hands-on learning welcome, and was convinced that the only way to learn to code, is to code. The spring of 2018 was an immersive experience into the world of software development, and resulted in core understanding and an eagerness for life-long learning!

The accelerated learning program was focused around Java, but covered a number of other areas as well. I learned server-side programming, database handling, front end development, agile product development, UX-design and cloud-computing. I was also active in making use of Helsinki University's massive open online courses on CS, and after the program took the open online course "Elements of AI" by Reaktor and Helsinki University. I am passionate about learning. My plan is to continue perfecting my coding skills and hope the future holds an opportunity to be able to some day call myself a full stack web developer.

Train traffic application

This was an application using real time train traffic information from Finnish Transportation Agency's open data service Digitraffic. The application provides a text-based user interface for searching train information. You can search information by train number or by station (all departing trains from a specific station, or all trains running between two stations). You can also search a specific train's geographical location by coordinates or location by which stations it has passed if it's currently running. The application covers both long-distance trains and commuter trains. This was a group project for an accelerated learning program in Java, accomplished by a group of four people in three days, with three weeks of teaching behind us.
